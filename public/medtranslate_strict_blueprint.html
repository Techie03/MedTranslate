01

Medical Safety Sentinel™

Category: Patient Safety · Moat Level: Maximum · Competitors with this: 0
Build First
What it does

A secondary AI model that runs on every translation output before display, checking against a medical safety ruleset: dosage alterations, allergy inversions, drug contraindication drops, negation errors (translating "no fever" as "fever"), and critical condition mistranslations. Flags, blocks, or forces human review. Generates a "Clinical Safety Score" (0–100) per translation.

Technical Build Spec

Stack: Custom NLI (Natural Language Inference) classifier fine-tuned on clinical error corpus + RAG over FDA Adverse Event Reporting System + MedDRA + SNOMED-CT. Latency target: <800ms secondary pass. Flagging threshold: configurable per clinical context (ICU vs. routine). Output: JSON safety object with flag type, severity, affected term, and recommended action.

Revenue / Hospital Value

This feature converts MedTranslate AI from a translation tool into a patient safety system. Hospital CIOs can cite this in Joint Commission audits. Risk management departments have measurable liability reduction. Pricing justification: hospitals pay $200K–$500K/year for patient safety software. This feature enables premium tier pricing. Also creates the proprietary training data flywheel that competitors cannot access.

02

Real-Time Bidirectional Voice Translation

Category: Clinical Encounter · Moat Level: High · Competitors with this: Mabel AI (no LLM), Jaide Health (limited)
Build Q1
What it does

Doctor and patient speak freely in their own languages. The system transcribes in real-time, translates, and speaks back the translation with <2 second latency. Displays text transcript for both parties simultaneously. Medical entity highlighting (drugs, conditions, symptoms) in real-time. Runs on iOS/Android native app. Session is recorded, encrypted, and attached to patient record.

Technical Build Spec

Stack: Whisper large-v3 streaming (faster-whisper) → medical NER (extracted entities) → domain-adapted MT model → TTS (Azure Neural Voice or ElevenLabs). Pipeline latency budget: STT 400ms + translation 600ms + TTS 500ms = 1.5s total. Speaker diarization via pyannote.audio to separate doctor/patient turns. HIPAA: all audio processed on-device or AWS GovCloud, never stored by default. Optional session logging with explicit consent.

Revenue / Hospital Value

Replaces $60–$100/hour video remote interpretation costs. A hospital with 500 language-barrier encounters/month at $75/hour on-demand interpreter = $450K/year in interpreter costs. MedTranslate at $150K/year with unlimited encounters = $300K annual saving + better response times. This ROI calculation closes contracts. Target: 40% of all encounters shift to AI-assisted within 6 months of deployment.

03

Translation Confidence + Explainability Engine

Category: Clinical Trust · Moat Level: High · Competitors with this: 0
Build Q1
What it does

Every translated sentence carries a calibrated confidence score (0–100%) with a color-coded visual indicator (green/amber/red). Clicking any amber or red sentence shows WHY it was uncertain: ambiguous source term, no target-language medical equivalent, low-frequency training data, or detected negation/conditional structure. A "Review Required" threshold automatically routes low-confidence outputs to a human interpreter queue.

Technical Build Spec

Stack: Calibrated translation model with Monte Carlo dropout uncertainty sampling OR ensemble of 3 MT models with variance scoring. Attention map extraction for term-level uncertainty attribution. Medical knowledge graph lookup to detect untranslatable concepts. Output schema: {sentence_id, score, flags[], affected_terms[], recommended_action}. Integration with human interpreter dispatch queue via webhook.

Revenue / Hospital Value

This feature is the answer to the #1 physician objection: "How do I know I can trust this?" 57% of physicians plan AI translation adoption but have no quality standard to evaluate tools against. A visible, explainable confidence score is the trust bridge. It also enables tiered pricing: "Verified" translations (human-reviewed when AI confidence <80%) as a premium tier. Projected: 30% conversion uplift from demos to signed contracts.

Multilingual Discharge Summary Generator

Category: Patient Outcomes · Moat Level: Medium-High · Competitors with this: 0

What it does

At discharge, AI ingests the EHR encounter data (diagnosis, medications, follow-up instructions, warning signs) and generates a patient-facing summary in their language at a 6th-grade reading level. Includes medication schedule visual, follow-up appointment details, emergency warning signs in bold. Delivered as PDF, SMS, WhatsApp message, or printed. 06

Patient Comprehension Score (PCS)

Category: Outcomes Measurement · Moat Level: Maximum · Competitors with this: 0
Build Q2–Q3
What it does

After delivering a translated discharge instruction or medication explanation, AI presents 2–3 adaptive comprehension questions to the patient in their language (via voice or text). "When should you take this medication?" "What should you do if you feel chest pain?" AI scores responses, generates a Patient Comprehension Score (0–100), and flags the encounter if score is below 70. Triggers automatic re-explanation or interpreter escalation.

Technical Build Spec

LLM generates context-specific verification questions from instruction content → question delivered via TTS in patient language → patient responds by voice or text → LLM evaluates response against expected answer semantically (not exact match) → score computed → if score < threshold: branch to simplified re-explanation → log all interactions in FHIR Communication resource → score surfaced in clinician dashboard.

Revenue / Hospital Value

This is the feature that changes MedTranslate AI's product category. Competitors sell "translation delivered." MedTranslate AI sells "comprehension verified." In outcomes-based healthcare contracting, comprehension verification is worth far more than delivery confirmation. Health systems will pay a premium tier for this. It is also publishable in NEJM or JAMA as a clinical innovation, generating organic awareness worth millions in marketing budget equivalent.

Offline Emergency Mode (Edge AI)

Category: Emergency / Rural · Moat Level: High · Competitors with this: Mabel AI (phrase-lookup only)
Build Q3
What it does

A fully offline mode running on a tablet or phone with zero internet. Pre-loads a quantized LLM (capable of medical translation in 30 target languages) and a database of 2,000 critical medical phrases. Activates via a large red "Emergency Mode" button. Designed for ambulances, rural clinics, disaster response, ships, and flights. Interface stripped to maximum-contrast minimal mode. Syncs session to cloud when connectivity restored.

Technical Build Spec

Quantized model: Llama 3.1 8B in 4-bit GGUF format via llama.cpp (~4.5GB on device). Runtime: ONNX Runtime or llama.cpp on Android/iOS. Pre-loaded phrase DB: SQLite with 2,000 critical medical phrases × 30 languages. Sync: background queue with conflict resolution. UI: large-button emergency mode, voice-first, auto-activates when network quality drops below threshold. Battery optimization: inference throttling on low battery.

Revenue / Hospital Value

Emergency medical services (EMS) market is a distinct buyer with federal funding. Rural Health Clinics (4,000+ federally designated) receive specific funding for language access. This is a hardware + software bundle opportunity: pre-configured tablets with offline AI. Annual government contracts for rural health and EMS are $50K–$200K per region. Humanitarian NGO market (WHO, MSF, ICRC) adds non-dilutive grant revenue. PR value: "We work when the internet doesn't" is a story that generates coverage in every major tech and health publication.
